Innovation in Ed Tech

At the U.S. State Department’s English Language Programs, I co-built and launched the Virtual Educator initiative, scaling it to support over 650 online teaching projects in 62 countries. I led platform enhancements for program applicants and proposal submissions, streamlined workflows with project management tools, and introduced data dashboards that improved decision-making across teams.

Whether integrating LMS tools to enhance student outcomes or guiding educators in using immersive apps like Prezi Video and augmented reality, I focus on Ed Tech that centers the user and expands access to meaningful learning experiences.

Innovation in Strategy

In my work at the intersection of mission-driven strategy and global operations, I’ve helped organizations reimagine what's possible. I’ve led sustainability-focused consulting projects for the Norwegian Embassy and Seoul-based GS Energy, leveraging design thinking and cross-cultural collaboration to drive public engagement and lasting impact. I also advised an international nonprofit in shifting from grant dependency to a sustainable business model, enabling long-term revenue growth.

Whether through change management, business model innovation, or operational transformation, I take a systems-level approach to solving complex challenges with creativity and purpose.

Innovation in the Classroom

Innovation in the classroom doesn’t stop at the student—it starts with empowering educators. Across Indonesia and the U.S., I’ve designed and facilitated teacher training workshops that bring project-based learning, culturally responsive teaching, and educational technology into practice. I’ve trained over 2,600 educators on integrating multilingual children’s books into extensive reading programs, and led professional development sessions on tools like Prezi Video and Google Jamboard to boost student engagement.

At the same time, I’ve developed hands-on, learner-centered experiences for university and adult learners that reflect community voices and lived experiences. For me, classroom innovation means building the capacity of both teachers and students to think critically, connect meaningfully, and lead with creativity.
